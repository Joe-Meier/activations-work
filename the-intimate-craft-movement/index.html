The Challenge

Fruit of the Loom faced declining relevance among conscious consumers who viewed mass-produced intimates as impersonal and environmentally questionable. The brand needed to reconnect with values-driven shoppers seeking authentic, sustainable alternatives to fast fashion undergarments.

The Insight

In an age of mass production, the most intimate garments should reflect our most personal values

"True intimacy starts with what touches you first"
The Idea

We transformed select Fruit of the Loom stores into artisanal weaving workshops, inviting customers to hand-craft their own underwear using locally-sourced organic cotton and hemp fibers. Master weavers guided participants through the meditative process of creating truly bespoke intimates, fostering deep emotional connections between maker and garment.
